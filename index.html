The Crow
Objective 1 · Crows
Crows are urban generalists. Their sighting frequency barely shifts between SF's quietest residential neighborhoods and its densest commercial corridors, 37% to 35%. They don't need Clement Street. They just don't avoid it. That's because food businesses create exactly the infrastructure crows exploit: dumpsters, outdoor dining scraps, bin collection days. The same density that drives jays out (noise, foot traffic, reduced canopy) functions as a food subsidy for crows. They are commensal: they benefit from human activity without depending on any particular form of it. Jays are the opposite. Their frequency drops 2.5x between low- and high-commercial neighborhoods. Same family, opposite strategy.
